Design

Color Theory

In the first lecture for design we had a lesson on colors and how to use them correctly. We talked about things like primary, secondary and tertiary colors, as well as the color wheel, this lecture also introduced me to a terms like hue, tint, tone and shade. On top of that while coming up with my portfolio I delved into color schemes which led to a rabbit whole where I got my first taste of analo-gous and complimentary color schemes

conclusion:

This lecture was one of the most fun ones I have ever had and it thought me a lot about what color compliments what and where to use them.

Image formating

During this lectures we learned about image formatting and the different us-es for each image format. The main takeaway which I got was that when editing its best to use png instead of jpg since pngs save more of the data in the picture and once compressed the data is gone which makes the picture look pixelated.

We also talked a bit about video formats and we compared the different vid-eo formats in class in order to find out which one is the best for what purpose. And after the discussions we decided that AV1 and H.264 are the way to go.

conclusion:

Knowing which image format greatly helps with creating websites that look nice and load fast.

Magazines and layouts

In my media production class, we had this cool lecture about designing posters and magazines. The main thing I learned is that picking the right fonts, styles, and organizing letters is super important in making a design look awesome. They also talked about using a grid layout for magazines, which basically means setting up a kind of framework to make sure everything in the magazine looks great and fits well. It's like having a plan to make sure every part of the magazine is used the best way it can be!

conclusion:

This project taught me that design basics such as grids, hierarchy, whitespace, and alignment are crucial. They play a big part in making a magazine layout look organized and professional. Working on it, I learned about the careful balance needed to create a design that's both visually appealing and functional. These fundamentals act as the foundation for a well-structured magazine layout.
